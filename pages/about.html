Vision

ABLE aims to facilitate the use of interactive, educational gaming applications that provide supplementary assistance for the learning and review processes that take place outside of the classroom. The ultimate vision is to supply a variety of entertaining, informative game-based programs that both students and instructors will want to use. The main goal of this project is to develop these programs and make them widely available for little or no cost to use.

Overview

Applications for Basic Learning and Education (ABLE) is a web-based collection of interactive educational programs that provide engaging and entertaining ways to supplement learning outside of the classroom.

Educational institutions, extracurricular tutoring organizations, and involved parents are constantly searching for new ways to assist learning and reviewing material beyond the restraints of regular school hours. This search is made far more difficult by students’ reluctance to participate in activities that they perceive as being tedious or overly time-consuming.

Over the years various educational games have been created to fulfill this need for tasks that are both interesting and informative. The computer-based educational game market seemed to peak in the 1990s, with numerous learning-based software applications being sold primarily in the form of CDs. However, as the Internet became more accessible and information became more readily available, both the quantity and quality of educational applications experienced a severe decline.

A few major businesses, such as Leapfrog and The Learning Company, still focus on producing educational games; however, the relative rarity of these programs, the market’s desire for them, and the level of work needed to produce a successful product often combine to cause these applications to have very high consumer prices. Additionally, the majority of educational games currently available are geared specifically towards young children who are just beginning their educations and focus on only one subject, such as mathematics or reading skills, per application.

The primary focus of ABLE is to address these pain points by making high-quality educational games which are easily accessible to the public. We will accomplish this by being web-based, which allows for ABLE to be accessed from an immeasurable number of different devices and locations, and by making general use of ABLE free to the public. Upon completion ABLE will possess a wide variety of learning-based applications that range in topics from general learning skills to specific subjects. ABLE will also cater to far more varied age ranges, with games directed for almost any level of education.

We develop our software using free and open source technologies to minimize the cost of production. Additional profit will be gained upon completion of the project by making certain features only available to users who have memberships or through donations.
